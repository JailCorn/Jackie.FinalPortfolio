Magazine Cover Mockup


I designed this in my Art 115D Digital Art course during my last semester at Leeward Community College in Fall 2021. This class taught me pretty much all of the fundamentals of design such as the elements/principles of design, grids, the golden ratio, etc. I enjoyed this class and I created a lot of work that I'm still proud of today!

I'm still proud of this fake magazine cover I designed. I took the picture of myself using my phone and my own trumpet! I still think I did a good job with the layout, colors, and font choice. Desgining this reinforced the love I have for music and made me appreciate the craftsmanship of the instruments I own.


Magazine Cover Process


Unfortunately, I don't have the original files of this process work, and I just have this screenshot of my drafts. I actually did an "Instrumental" magazine cover back in high school, so I viewed this project as a chance to improve on the poster I did back then. The first draft on the far left is what my high school poster looked like, just with a slightly different layout and different fonts.

This assignment was during a time where I was just flowing with ideas, so I had a lot of fun drafting these out and writing the copy. I even think these drafts are pretty good on their own. As a musician myself, I owned these instruments and planned to take the photos myself. I'm really happy with how the photos came out, as you can see in the completed version!

This assignment was pretty straightforward for me. I let the ideas flow and didn't really have much difficulty executing them.
